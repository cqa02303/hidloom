# HIDloom Community Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

HIDloom maintainers and contributors commit to a respectful, inclusive, and harassment-free community. Participation
must remain safe regardless of identity, background, experience, ability, appearance, nationality, or beliefs.

## Expected behavior

- Be considerate, technically constructive, and respectful of different experience levels.
- Critique ideas and code rather than people.
- Accept correction, acknowledge mistakes, and help repair their impact.
- Protect private information and use sanitized examples, logs, and screenshots.
- Prefer decisions that improve the health of the whole community.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Harassment, threats, discrimination, stalking, or unwanted sexual attention;
- insults, trolling, personal attacks, or sustained disruption;
- publishing private or identifying information without permission;
- retaliation against someone who reports a concern;
- conduct that a reasonable person would consider unsafe or inappropriate in a professional community.

## Scope and enforcement

This policy applies in project repositories, issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, and events, and when someone
officially represents HIDloom elsewhere. Maintainers may edit or remove content, close interactions, issue warnings,
temporarily restrict participation, or permanently ban participants. Responses should be proportionate, documented,
and protect reporters' privacy.

To report conduct privately, follow the private-contact procedure in [`SECURITY.md`](SECURITY.md) and identify the
request as a Code of Conduct report. Do not publish incident details in a public issue. If private contact is not yet
available, open a public issue containing only a request for private maintainer contact.

## Attribution

This policy follows the principles and enforcement structure of the
[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct.html), with project-specific reporting instructions.
